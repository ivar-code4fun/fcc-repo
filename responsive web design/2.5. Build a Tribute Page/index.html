Richard Feynman

The man who truely understood Quantum Mechanics

Here's a time line of Feynmans life:

  • 1918 - Richard Feynman is born in Manhattan, New York to Melville Feynman and Lucille Phillips.
  • 1939 - Richard Feynman earns a B.Sc from Massachusetts Institute of Technology(MIT). MIT is where he found his interest in Theoretical Physics.
  • 1941-1942 - Richard Feynman works on the atomic bomb project at Princeton with Albert Einstein.
  • 1942 - Richard Feynman graduates from Princeton and earns a PhD.
  • 1945 - Richard Feynman is taught physics at Cornell.
  • 1961 - Richard Feynman releases his first of 10 books.
  • 1965 - Richard Feynman wins the Nobel Prize in physics for "their fundamental work in quantum electrodynamics, with deep-ploughing consequences for the physics of elementary particles."
  • 1988 - Richard Feynman passes away at the age of 69 in Los Angeles, California.
